About Me

I trained as an English teacher, where I spent the first few years of my career. I ended up teaching coding and pioneering our school's first AP program. When COVID hit, I trained as a software developer. Those years in the tech industry led me to a proficiency in research and a curiosity for the unknown. I've since found my niche in technical writing.

I have over 12 years of experience as an exceptionally precise writer and editor, as well as over 5 years in the technology sector. My ability to synthesize and present abstract technical concepts, using tools like Word, PowerPoint, and Visio, as well as through curriculum creation and live presentations, sets me apart from other technical writers.

In addition, I independently manage and organize multiple documentation pipelines at once. I can quickly pivot and adapt as situations and priorities change.

My great people skills and approachable, analytical personality lend itself well to communicating with highly-technical individuals. I have a high value for all people, and that respect wins trust and helps create an enjoyable working environment for everyone.

My sharp eye for organization, as well as my ability to grasp and communicate a high level of subject-matter knowledge, make me an ideal technical writer. And when deadlines are looming, I am always calm.

Services

A brief snapshot of some of my top abilities.

Technical Writing

Autonomously researching specific skills, concepts, and functionality, to create and update technical documentation. I've created comprehensive documents that explain a concept in its entirety, mediumm-length guides that explain what is useful for most users, and short, how-to guides that prioritize brevity. I've also written release notes to coincide with software updates.

Editing

My committment to accuracy dates back to my years as a university student, where I worked part-time as a writing tutor at the university's writing center. I also edited countless student papers and publications articles. When I'm not at work, I can't help but notice the misspellings, grammar mistakes, and inconsistent spacing that so often occurs on billboards and signs.

Presentations

As a seasoned educator, I have seven years of experience presenting and training over a variety of topics. I am quite comfortable and polished in front of a crowd. In addition, I have a lot of experience optimizing learner engagement and retention in alignment with specific learning goals.

LMS Administration

One of my jobs as a technical writer was specifically for a SaaS company that offered a LMS/LXP to their customers. My principal responsibility was researching and writing about how their LMS worked. I am quite comfortable as an administrator, since that is how I gained an understanding of their system: I created various user accounts with different access levels, then I logged in as those users to test various functionality that I had set up.

Copywriting

I taught high school publications, where I taught journalism, modeled copywriting, actively practiced copyediting, taught page and book design, and photography. Afterward, I freelanced as a copywrighter, proposal writer, and nonfiction writer. When I was attending university, I also served on the editorial staff of my university's literary magazine, SPIRITS.

Research

I fell in love with coding when I got a job teaching computer science; a field I had no previous experience in. That experience taught me that I have a high ability to learn new ideas quickly. Throughout my career as a technical writer, I've enthusiastically embraced a fearless curiosity of the unknown. I bring that same drive to all of my positions.
